## Design Context

### Users

PoliceConduct.org serves local residents, defense attorneys, journalists,
advocates, and civic researchers who need to understand law-enforcement records
for a specific geography or agency. Users may arrive stressed, skeptical, or
time-constrained. They need fast paths to local agencies, personnel history,
public reports, civil litigation, and source coverage without having to infer
how the data model works.

### Brand Personality

The interface should feel sober, credible, civic, and public-interest oriented.
It should communicate care, restraint, and evidence-backed transparency rather
than outrage, hype, or promotional confidence. Positive findings are allowed
only when they are evidence-backed and clearly scoped.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use a light-mode-first civic/editorial dashboard style: restrained navy text,
thin rules, compact tables, measured spacing, muted teal and deep red accents
where useful, and clear hierarchy. Prefer dense but scannable research surfaces
over marketing layouts. Civic index pages should feel like practical public
records tools, not landing pages.

Avoid gradients, glassmorphism, decorative blobs, sensational language, dark
neon dashboards, nested cards, generic AI metric walls, and placeholder copy
that exposes internal data collection gaps. When data is not available, show an
empty measurement or neutral placeholder such as `$--` rather than language that
announces missing data.

### Design Principles

- Make the primary next action obvious. For geography pages, the default action
  is usually drilling into child entities: counties/areas, places, or agencies.
- Keep route and data identity visible through user-facing paths and labels, but
  never expose implementation mechanics or collection caveats as product copy.
- Prioritize residents and legal researchers: fast local lookup, credible record
  summaries, searchable lists, and clear paths to personnel and civil cases.
- Use charts only when they answer a real civic question. Empty graphs are
  acceptable as neutral measurement space, but they should not overwhelm the
  page or imply false precision.
- Preserve shared navigation, breadcrumbs, and database-backed URL conventions
  across all civic index pages.
